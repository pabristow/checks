Luhn algorithm

Description

The Luhn algorithm is used with a lot of codes and numbers: the most well-known usage is the verification of the credit card numbers It produces a check digit from a sequence with an unlimited length. The weight pattern used is from the rightmost digit (the check digit) doubling the value of every second digit. It applies a modulus 10 on the sum, so the range of the check digit is from 0 to 9.

Note

When a digit is doubled, we subtract 9 from the result if it exceeds 9

Errors

Alterations of any one digit are all caught. The alterations of more than one digit are not all caught. All transpositions on digits with different weight are caught, but the sequence "90" or "09" is not because: 

9*2 = 18 and 18-9 = 9 
9 * 2 = 9 * 1
0 * 2 = 0 * 1

The two digits have the same value if they are doubled or not. Because Luhn alternates a weight of 1 and 2, any transpositions of digits with the same weight are not caught.
